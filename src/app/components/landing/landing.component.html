Agarathi is a Tamil word

“Data Agarathi” is a product that is radically different to most Metadata tools in the market. It is based on following fundamental principles:
• All data in an organization needs to be described. Selectively describing some of the data based on business importance, regulatory needs or any other criteria results in wasted money and effort because business needs can’t be confined to criteria known at any point in time.

• We preach and adapt an Active Metadata approach where Data is described when the data is created in the first place. Just like a car can’t be produced without specifications of every component it is going to use. A car is not first manufactured and then its specifications documented. Similarly, a piece of data, which is an asset, must be created to a specification.

• A Data product cannot be fully utilized without having all three types of Metadata integrated. Our product integrates Business, Technical and Operational Metadata.

Agarathi provides the tooling necessary to capture, integrate, maintain and publish Metadata. Unlike traditional notion of Metadata being a post event documentation, we advocate controlling processes with Metadata by adapting “Active Metadata” approach.

Business Metadata

All data assets that are defined, created and consumed directly by business are within the scope of Business Metadata. The assets can be atomic attributes are grouped together to create a “Data Product”, which is also an asset. We call this Data Product a Business Object.

Operational Metadata

Operational data can be described as data that is needed to initiate, control and complete Data Management processes. Metadata describing Operational data is Operational Metadata. Most Metadata management tools in the market do not have the capability to harvest, integrate and store operational metadata.

There are two kinds of Operational Metadata. There are those that are input to the processes or their control mechanisms. For example, Job Scheduling Metadata is an input to the data ingestion and computation processes. Whereas Logs produced by the Ingestion and Computation jobs can be treated as Operational Metadata.

Technical Metadata

Traditionally, this metadata is better managed in most organisations. Database Schema, File specifications, Message Pay Load definitions, API documentation are some of the examples of Technical Metadata. One key differentiator that Data Agarathi provides is the integration between Business Metadata and Technical Metadata. This enables technical events to be seen with a business lense. For example, instead of “JOB ID XXCCVV-001” completed successfully, we can now say “Customer Master data has been loaded successfully”.
